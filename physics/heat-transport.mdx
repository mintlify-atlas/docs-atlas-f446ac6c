## Overview

Heat transport redistributes energy across the moon's surface through two mechanisms:

1. **Conduction**: Always present, represents slow diffusive heat flow
2. **Convection**: Activated above threshold temperature, represents fast advective transport

Terrain features (mountains, slopes, ocean currents) modify transport efficiency using pre-computed directional coefficients.

## Transport Coefficient

### Temperature-Dependent Activation

Total heat transport combines baseline conduction with thermally-activated convection:

**Convection Activation** (`compute_convection_activation`):

$$
\text{activation}(T) = \frac{1}{2}\left(1 + \tanh\left(\frac{T - T_{\text{conv}}}{w_{\text{conv}}}\right)\right)
$$

**Total Transport Coefficient** (`compute_heat_transport_coefficient`):

$$
k_{\text{heat}}(T) = k_{\text{base}} + k_{\text{conv}} \cdot \text{activation}(T)
$$

where:
- $k_{\text{base}} = 15$ W·m⁻²·K⁻¹ (conduction - always present)
- $k_{\text{conv}} = 30$ W·m⁻²·K⁻¹ (convection - temperature-activated)
- $T_{\text{conv}} = 270$ K (activation threshold)
- $w_{\text{conv}} = 20$ K (transition width)

<Note>
Convection doubles the transport rate in warm regions (T > 290 K) compared to cold regions (T < 250 K). This creates a nonlinear feedback: hot regions transport heat more efficiently, helping distribute solar energy.
</Note>

**Example Values**:

| Temperature | Activation | $k_{\text{heat}}$ | Regime |
|-------------|------------|-------------------|--------|
| 250 K (-23°C) | 0.01 | 15.3 W·m⁻²·K⁻¹ | Conduction only |
| 270 K (-3°C) | 0.50 | 30.0 W·m⁻²·K⁻¹ | Transitional |
| 290 K (17°C) | 0.99 | 44.7 W·m⁻²·K⁻¹ | Full convection |
| 310 K (37°C) | 1.00 | 45.0 W·m⁻²·K⁻¹ | Maximum transport |

## Terrain-Based Transport Modifiers

### Directional Coefficients

Heat transport between cells is modified by terrain features. Transport coefficients are pre-computed at initialization and stored in a 3D array:

```julia
transport_coeffs[i, j, dir]  # dir ∈ {1=North, 2=South, 3=East, 4=West}
```

**Source Code**: `src/physics/terrain.jl:85-117`

### Slope Barrier Effect

Steep terrain blocks heat transport regardless of direction:

$$
f_{\text{barrier}}(\Delta z) = \frac{1}{1 + s_{\text{barrier}} \cdot (\Delta z)^2}
$$

where:
- $\Delta z = z_{\text{neighbor}} - z_{\text{self}}$ (elevation difference)
- $s_{\text{barrier}} = 4.0$ (slope barrier strength)

<Note>
The quadratic dependence $(\Delta z)^2$ means barriers are symmetric: uphill and downhill transport are equally blocked. A mountain ridge blocks heat flow in both directions.
</Note>

### Downslope Flow Asymmetry

Heat flows more easily downhill than uphill:

$$
f_{\text{asymmetry}}(\Delta z) = 1 - s_{\text{down}} \cdot \tanh(\Delta z \cdot s_{\text{scale}})
$$

where:
- $s_{\text{down}} = 0.4$ (downslope strength)
- $s_{\text{scale}} = 3.0$ (slope scale)

**Interpretation**:
- $\Delta z > 0$: Neighbor is higher → flowing downhill → $f > 1$ (easier)
- $\Delta z < 0$: Neighbor is lower → flowing uphill → $f < 1$ (harder)
- $\Delta z = 0$: Flat terrain → $f = 1$ (neutral)

### Combined Transport Modifier

**Directional Heat Transport** (`compute_directional_heat_transport_modifier`):

$$
m_{\text{heat}}(z_{\text{self}}, z_{\text{neighbor}}) = f_{\text{barrier}}(\Delta z) \cdot f_{\text{asymmetry}}(\Delta z)
$$

**Source Code**: `src/physics/terrain.jl:56-66`

### Ocean Transport Bonus

Ocean currents transport heat more efficiently than land:

$$
m_{\text{ocean}}(z) = \begin{cases}
1.5 & \text{if } z < 0 \text{ (ocean)} \\
1.0 & \text{if } z \geq 0 \text{ (land)}
\end{cases}
$$

**Final Transport Coefficient**:

$$
k_{ij} = k_{\text{heat}}\left(\frac{T_i + T_j}{2}\right) \cdot m_{\text{ocean}}(z_i) \cdot m_{\text{heat}}(z_i, z_j)
$$

where $k_{\text{heat}}$ depends on the average temperature between cells.

## Implementation Notes

### Numerical Flux Computation

For a cell $(i,j)$ exchanging heat with its neighbors:

$$
\frac{dQ}{dt}\bigg|_{\text{transport}} = \sum_{\text{neighbors}} k_{ij} \cdot (T_j - T_i) \cdot A / d
$$

where:
- $A$ = cell area
- $d$ = distance between cell centers
- $k_{ij}$ = modified transport coefficient

### Pre-computation Strategy

Transport coefficients are calculated once during moon initialization:

```julia
function populate_heat_transport_coefficients!(transport_coeffs, elevation, n_lat, n_lon)
    for i in 1:n_lat, j in 1:n_lon
        elev_self = elevation[i, j]
        ocean_mult = compute_ocean_transport_multiplier(elev_self)
        
        # Compute for all 4 directions
        # North, South, East (wrapped), West (wrapped)
    end
end
```

**Source Code**: `src/physics/terrain.jl:85-117`

<Note>
Pre-computing transport coefficients saves ~40% computation time in the ODE solver by avoiding repeated terrain queries. The coefficients are terrain-dependent but temperature-independent (the temperature-dependent part $k_{\text{heat}}(T)$ is computed dynamically).
</Note>
