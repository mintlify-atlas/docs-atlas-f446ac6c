## Overview

The radiative transfer module implements energy balance physics for incoming solar radiation and outgoing longwave radiation. Key features include:

- Ice-albedo feedback with smooth transitions
- Greenhouse effect with water vapor coupling
- Elevation-dependent atmospheric opacity
- Eclipse handling during orbital transit

## Solar Absorption

### Incoming Solar Flux

The Hot Moon receives intense stellar radiation:

```julia
SOLAR_CONSTANT = 2100.0  # W/m² (1.54× Earth)
```

Incoming flux depends on solar geometry (see [Solar Geometry](/api/solar-geometry)):

$$
Q_{\text{in}} = S_0 \cdot (1 - \tau_{\text{solar}}) \cdot \cos(\theta_z) \cdot (1 - \alpha)
$$

where:
- $S_0$ = solar constant (2100 W/m²)
- $\tau_{\text{solar}}$ = atmospheric solar optical depth (0.25)
- $\theta_z$ = solar zenith angle
- $\alpha$ = surface albedo (temperature-dependent)

### Albedo and Ice-Albedo Feedback

Surface albedo varies with temperature through ice coverage:

**Ice Fraction Calculation** (`compute_ice_fraction_from_temperature`):

$$
f_{\text{ice}} = \frac{1}{2}\left(1 - \tanh\left(\frac{T - T_f}{w_{\text{ice}}}\right)\right)
$$

**Surface Albedo** (`compute_surface_albedo`):

$$
\alpha(T) = \alpha_{\text{base}} \cdot (1 - f_{\text{ice}}) + \alpha_{\text{ice}} \cdot f_{\text{ice}}
$$

<Note>
The `tanh` transition ensures numerical stability and smooth derivatives for ODE solvers. The ice fraction transitions smoothly around the freezing point rather than having a discontinuous jump.
</Note>

**Parameter Values**:

| Parameter | Symbol | Value | Unit |
|-----------|--------|-------|------|
| Base albedo | $\alpha_{\text{base}}$ | 0.28 | - |
| Ice albedo | $\alpha_{\text{ice}}$ | 0.60 | - |
| Freezing point | $T_f$ | 273.15 | K |
| Ice transition width | $w_{\text{ice}}$ | 10.0 | K |
| Solar optical depth | $\tau_{\text{solar}}$ | 0.25 | - |

**Example**: At 263 K (-10°C):
- Ice fraction: ~0.84 (mostly frozen)
- Albedo: ~0.55 (highly reflective)

## Greenhouse Effect

### Infrared Optical Depth

The atmosphere absorbs outgoing longwave radiation based on total optical depth:

**Base Optical Depth** (`BASE_IR_OPTICAL_DEPTH`):

```julia
τ_base = 1.0  # Permanent gases (CO₂, etc.)
```

**Water Vapor Contribution** (`compute_water_vapor_optical_depth`):

$$
\tau_{\text{wv}} = \tau_{\text{scale}} \cdot \ln\left(1 + \frac{M}{M_{\text{ref}}}\right)
$$

where:
- $\tau_{\text{scale}}$ = 0.8 (water vapor optical scale)
- $M$ = column moisture (kg/m²)
- $M_{\text{ref}}$ = 5.0 kg/m² (reference moisture)

<Note>
Logarithmic scaling represents diminishing returns: doubling moisture doesn't double the greenhouse effect. This matches real atmospheric radiative transfer where absorption lines saturate.
</Note>

**Total Optical Depth** (`compute_total_ir_optical_depth`):

$$
\tau_{\text{total}} = \tau_{\text{base}} + \tau_{\text{wv}}(M)
$$

**Elevation Adjustment** (`compute_effective_optical_depth_at_elevation`):

High-altitude locations have thinner atmospheres above them:

$$
\tau_{\text{eff}}(z) = \tau_{\text{total}} \cdot \left(1 - \gamma_{\text{elev}} \cdot \max(0, z)\right)
$$

where $\gamma_{\text{elev}} = 0.5$ (elevation greenhouse reduction factor).

### Transmissivity and Outgoing Radiation

**Eddington Approximation** (`compute_ir_transmissivity_from_optical_depth`):

$$
t_{\text{IR}} = \frac{2}{2 + \tau}
$$

This approximation balances simplicity with physical realism:
- $\tau = 0$: $t = 1.0$ (transparent, no greenhouse effect)
- $\tau = 1$: $t = 0.67$ (moderate absorption)
- $\tau = 2$: $t = 0.5$ (strong greenhouse effect)
- $\tau \to \infty$: $t \to 0$ (opaque)

**Outgoing Longwave Radiation** (`compute_outgoing_longwave_radiation`):

$$
Q_{\text{out}} = \varepsilon \cdot \sigma \cdot T^4 \cdot t_{\text{IR}}
$$

where:
- $\varepsilon$ = 0.95 (surface emissivity)
- $\sigma$ = 5.67×10⁻⁸ W·m⁻²·K⁻⁴ (Stefan-Boltzmann constant)
- $T$ = surface temperature (K)
- $t_{\text{IR}}$ = infrared transmissivity

## Implementation Details

All radiative functions use `@inline` and `@fastmath` macros for performance:

```julia
@inline @fastmath function compute_ice_fraction_from_temperature(T::Real)
    x = (T - FREEZING_POINT) / ICE_TRANSITION_WIDTH
    return 0.5 * (1 - tanh(x))
end
```
