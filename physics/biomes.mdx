## Overview

The biome system classifies each grid cell into one of 12 biome types based on temperature, moisture, and elevation. Each biome has a characteristic heat capacity that affects thermal inertia and climate response timescales.

Biomes serve two purposes:
1. **Physical**: Determine heat capacity for energy balance calculations
2. **Visualization**: Color-code climate maps for analysis

<Note>
Heat capacity varies by 67× between extremes (hot desert: 5×10⁵ vs ocean: 2×10⁷). High heat capacity biomes (ocean, wetlands, forests) resist temperature changes and smooth diurnal/seasonal cycles, while low heat capacity biomes (deserts, mountains) respond rapidly to radiative forcing.
</Note>

## Classification Thresholds

### Temperature Zones

**Temperature Boundaries**:

| Zone | Temperature Range | Constant |
|------|-------------------|----------|
| Ice | T < 263.15 K (-10°C) | `BIOME_T_ICE` |
| Tundra | 263.15 K ≤ T < 273.15 K | `BIOME_T_TUNDRA` |
| Cold | 273.15 K ≤ T < 283.15 K | `BIOME_T_COLD` |
| Temperate | 283.15 K ≤ T < 293.15 K | `BIOME_T_TEMPERATE` |
| Hot | T ≥ 293.15 K (20°C) | - |

**Transition Width**: 5.0 K (`BIOME_T_WIDTH`)

### Moisture Zones

**Moisture Boundaries**:

| Zone | Moisture Range | Constant |
|------|----------------|----------|
| Very Dry | M < 2.0 kg/m² | `BIOME_M_DESERT` |
| Dry | M < 3.0 kg/m² | `BIOME_M_DRY` |
| Moderate | M < 4.0 kg/m² | `BIOME_M_MODERATE` |
| Wet | M ≥ 4.0 kg/m² | - |
| Very Wet | M ≥ 5.0 kg/m² | `BIOME_M_WET` |

**Transition Width**: 1.0 kg/m² (`BIOME_M_WIDTH`)

### Elevation Zones

| Zone | Elevation Range | Constant |
|------|-----------------|----------|
| Ocean | z < 0 | - |
| Wetland | 0 ≤ z < 0.1 | `BIOME_ELEV_WETLAND` |
| Land | 0.1 ≤ z < 0.5 | - |
| Mountain | z ≥ 0.5 | `BIOME_ELEV_MOUNTAIN` |

**Transition Width**: 0.05 (`BIOME_ELEV_WIDTH`)

## Smooth Biome Blending

For physical calculations (not visualization), heat capacity is **smoothly blended** using hyperbolic tangent transitions rather than hard boundaries.

### Zone Weight Functions

**Ice Zone Weight** (`compute_ice_zone_weight`):

$$
w_{\text{ice}}(T) = \frac{1}{2}\left(1 - \tanh\left(\frac{T - T_{\text{ice}}}{w_T}\right)\right)
$$

**Tundra Zone Weight** (`compute_tundra_zone_weight`):

$$
w_{\text{tundra}}(T) = \frac{1}{2}\left(1 + \tanh\left(\frac{T - T_{\text{ice}}}{w_T}\right)\right) \cdot \frac{1}{2}\left(1 - \tanh\left(\frac{T - T_{\text{tundra}}}{w_T}\right)\right)
$$

Similarly for cold, temperate, and hot zones. **All weights sum to 1.0**.

**Source Code**: `src/physics/biomes.jl:6-69`

### Moisture-Dependent Blending

Within each temperature zone, biomes blend based on moisture:

**Cold Zone** (0-10°C):

$$
C_{\text{cold}} = w_{\text{wet}} \cdot C_{\text{boreal}} + (1 - w_{\text{wet}}) \cdot C_{\text{steppe}}
$$

**Temperate Zone** (10-20°C):

$$
C_{\text{temp}} = w_{\text{wet}} \cdot C_{\text{temp\_forest}} + (1 - w_{\text{wet}}) \cdot C_{\text{grassland}}
$$

**Hot Zone** (> 20°C) - three-way blend:

$$
C_{\text{hot}} = w_{\text{forest}} \cdot C_{\text{tropical}} + w_{\text{savanna}} \cdot C_{\text{savanna}} + w_{\text{desert}} \cdot C_{\text{desert}}
$$

where weights are based on desert/wet moisture thresholds.

**Source Code**: `src/physics/biomes.jl:126-158`

### Complete Heat Capacity Calculation

**Ocean Cells** (z < 0):

$$
C = C_{\text{ocean}} \cdot \text{depth\_factor}
$$

where depth_factor = `clamp(1.0 - z, 1.0, 2.0)` (deeper ocean = higher capacity).

**Mountain Cells** (z > 0.5):

$$
C = C_{\text{mountain}}
$$

**Land Cells**:

$$
C = w_{\text{mountain}} \cdot C_{\text{mountain}} + (1 - w_{\text{mountain}}) \cdot C_{\text{land\_blend}}
$$

where $C_{\text{land\_blend}}$ combines wetland and climate-zone heat capacities.

**Function**: `compute_biome_blended_heat_capacity(T, M, elevation)` (`src/physics/biomes.jl:180-201`)

<Note>
Smooth blending eliminates discontinuous derivatives that would cause ODE solver instability. A cell at the tundra/cold boundary smoothly interpolates between biome heat capacities as temperature changes, rather than jumping abruptly.
</Note>

## Discrete Classification (Visualization)

For visualization and analysis, cells are assigned to discrete biome IDs:

```julia
function classify_cell_biome(T, M, elevation)
    # Priority order: ocean → mountain → wetland → climate zones
    if elevation < 0.0
        return BIOME_OCEAN
    elseif elevation > BIOME_ELEV_MOUNTAIN
        return BIOME_MOUNTAIN
    elseif elevation < BIOME_ELEV_WETLAND
        return BIOME_WETLAND
    end
    
    # Climate zone classification
    if T < BIOME_T_ICE
        return BIOME_ICE_SHEET
    elseif T < BIOME_T_TUNDRA
        return BIOME_TUNDRA
    # ... (see source for complete logic)
end
```

## Heat Capacity Effects on Climate

### Diurnal Temperature Range

Low heat capacity biomes have large day-night temperature swings:

**Temperature change rate**:

$$
\frac{dT}{dt} = \frac{Q_{\text{net}}}{C}
$$

For the same radiative forcing $Q_{\text{net}}$:

| Biome | Heat Capacity | Relative Response Speed |
|-------|---------------|-------------------------|
| Hot Desert | 5.0×10⁵ | 40× faster than ocean |
| Mountain | 3.0×10⁵ | 67× faster than ocean |
| Grassland | 4.0×10⁶ | 5× faster than ocean |
| Tropical Forest | 8.0×10⁶ | 2.5× faster than ocean |
| Ocean | 2.0×10⁷ | 1× (baseline) |

<Note>
Deserts on the Hot Moon can experience temperature swings of 100+ K between subsolar and nightside points, while deep oceans maintain nearly constant temperatures throughout the rotation period.
</Note>

## Fallback Systems

### 1D Simulations (Latitude Only)

**Latitude-Based Heat Capacity** (`compute_latitude_based_heat_capacity`):

| Latitude Zone | Heat Capacity |
|---------------|---------------|
| Equatorial (\|lat\| < 30°) | 1.0×10⁶ J·m⁻²·K⁻¹ |
| Mid-latitude (30° ≤ \|lat\| < 70°) | 4.0×10⁶ |
| Polar (\|lat\| ≥ 70°) | 1.0×10⁷ |

**Source Code**: `src/physics/biomes.jl:237-251`

### 2D Temperature-Only Simulations

**Elevation-Based Heat Capacity** (`compute_elevation_based_heat_capacity`):

Combines latitude zones with elevation modifiers:
- Ocean: depth-scaled ocean capacity
- Wetland (0 < z < 0.15): linear interpolation to land
- Mountains (z > 0.5): transition to mountain capacity
- Land: latitude-based capacity
